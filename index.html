Rio de Janeiro

Morning

Rio is such an outdoors city!

We think your first stop should be the lagoon area, which you can get to from any crossing street on Rua Jardim Botânico. There is a running and bike path around it that is enticing for either a walk or a bike ride, and there are few places around where you can rent a bike. If your party is big, try those four seats ones for a different experience! As the afternoon fades away, head to Arpoador Beach (the very left side of Ipanema Beach) to go on the rock to enjoy the sunset from there. Cariocas (local people from Rio) love to watch the sunset from Arpoador Rock. They whistle and applause when the sun finally sets in the ocean (in the summer time) or behind the mountains (in the winter time). Another great place for the sunset is from Sugarloaf Mountain.

Evening

For dinner, try one of the Zona Sul Markets.

Believe it or not, they have one of the best pizzas in town. As you might guess, the ingredients are incredibly fresh and tasty. After dinner, and with your batteries recharged, head to the Lapa area to experience a local Samba club. The best options (from Tuesday to Saturday) are Rio Scenarium, Carioca da Gema and Lapa 40°. The best way to get in and out of Lapa is by taxi.

London

Morning

Time for a spot of shopping at Portobello Market, one the most famous street markets in the world.

Then take the tube to Oxford Circus. From here amble down Carnaby Street and through Soho to Greek Street, head to the Maison Bertaux for some delicious French fancies.

Evening

As evening draws in, it’s time to discover London’s world-famous West End. There are more than 40 theatres crammed into the square mile south of Oxford Street – and the only dilemma should be choosing exactly which production you want to see. The Lion King at the Lyceum? The Phantom of the Opera at Her Majesty’s Theatre in Haymarket? Mamma Mia!, The Book of Mormon, Kinky Boots, Wicked or Thriller Live…? Whichever you choose, a good time is guaranteed.

For dinner head to the Aqua Shard, 31 floors up, you can see enjoy spectacular views across the capital at night. Finish off your day with cocktails at the Beaufort Bar at the Savoy Hotel on the Strand. This art deco wonderland serves up exotic and delicious cocktails for a resolutely upmarket crowd. The bar itself stands on the hotel’s former cabaret stage (as graced by George Gershwin, among others) – and there are still nightly cabaret and burlesque shows.
